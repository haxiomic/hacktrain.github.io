DFT HACKS IS A HACKTRAIN INITIATIVE TO INNOVATE THE TRANSPORT INDUSTRY.

HackTrain is arguably the craziest and most memorable hackathon experiences on earth! A weekend-long adventure hacking whilst on moving trains! We invite a group of the best developers, designers and entrepreneurs from all around the world to come together to build mobile apps, websites, products and services to solve problems in the railway industry. All within 48 hours!!


THIS MARCH, WE HAVE BEEN COMMISSIONED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORT TO RUN DFT HACKS!!

This means different routes, challenges, datasets and venues, but the same crazy organisers!!! Pictures speak louder than words. Check out the aftermovie from our hackathons now!!

  • 1. What is a hackathon?

    A hackathon is a weekend long competition where designers, developers and entrepreneurs get together to collectively build prototypes that solve challenges using data, APIs and hardware!

  • 2. Can I apply?

    We welcome all programmers, designers and entrepreneurs! If you're really interested in innovating the transport industry then feel free to apply!

  • 3. Is experience required?

    No! We are open to coders, designers and entrepreneurs of all levels. You don’t need a CS degree to be a coder, or an Bachelor of Arts to be a designer!

  • 4. How do I apply?

    Go to apply.hacktrain.com to fill in an application form! The form has 4 sections for us to get to know you better. We aim to get back to you within a week after your application submission!

  • 5. When do applications close?

    Applications close on the 16th of March 2017!

  • 6. Are there any travel reimbursements provided?

    Yes there is! For applicants traveling from inside or outside the UK, we provide up to £25/£50 for travel reimbursement respectively. Reimbursements are limited and are given on a first come first serve basis to applicants, so apply early!

  • 7. What will I eat?

    We will provide you food from Friday’s lunch to Sunday’s lunch. If you have dietary restrictions, we’ll try our best to accommodate them. Don’t forget the abundance of snacks we’ll be providing throughout the hackathon weekend!

  • 8. Where will I sleep?

    On Friday night, you would be provided with hotel accommodation, but on the second night there will be none as the idea of a hackathon is to hack overnight for 48 hours! However, you can still rest when you need to!!

  • 9. How much does DfT Hacks cost?

    If you get through interview stage, the ticket would cost £25 which covers accommodation, food, travel during the hackathon and entry for the whole weekend!

  • 10. How are teams formed?

    Teams are formed on the first day. You can pitch your ideas for solutions to the challenges posed at the start of the event. You will each have a chance to share a 60-second informal pitch consisting of the problem you want to tackle, the solution you want to propose, and the team members you need to make it happen. The top ideas are chosen and the teams are formed.

  • 11. What gadgets will be provided to hack with?

    Raspberry Pis, arduinos, leap motion, iBeacons, drones, myo armbands, Amazon Echo and more!

  • 12. Where is the launch venue?

    The launch will be held at Department for Transport on Horseferry Rd! We will send you more details you have applied.

  • 13. Where’s the schedule?

    We will release a more detailed schedule in March. If you have applied, we will keep you updated at all times.

  • 14. What are the rules?

    You can review the Code of Conduct for the rules!

  • 15. How are the teams judged?

    You will have a chance to pitch your idea for 4 minutes + 2 mins Q&A from the judges! We split the judging criteria between 4 categories (each with it’s own percentage weighting): Business Model, MVP/Prototype, Design and Pitch/presentation. More detail will be sent to you through email before the hackathon

  • 16. When will I find out whether I have a ticket?

    After you have scheduled an interview, we aim to get back to you within a week on your interview results which should determine if you get a ticket.

  • 17. What would I have to bring for the DfTHacks weekend?

    Nothing but a laptop, clothes and toiletries for the weekend! And yourself!

  • 18. What if this FAQ doesn’t answer my questions?

    Feel free to email our Hackathon Talent gal, Sarah Chok, at sarah@hackpartners.com!
